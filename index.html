01 Foundations

What Is Performance Marketing?

Unlike brand marketing where you pay for impressions and hope for the best, performance marketing is pay-for-results. Every rupee spent is tied to a measurable action — a click, a lead, a sale, a download.

Core Idea Brand marketing builds the house. Performance marketing fills it with buyers. Both matter — but performance marketing gives you the scoreboard in real time.
PRINCIPLE 🎯

Measurability

Every campaign has a defined KPI. If you can't measure it, it doesn't count. Clicks, conversions, revenue, ROAS — all tracked in real time.

PRINCIPLE ⚡

Accountability

Ad spend is directly linked to outcomes. You pay when someone clicks (CPC), converts (CPA), or watches (CPV). No vague "awareness."

PRINCIPLE 🔄

Optimisation Loop

Launch → Measure → Learn → Adjust → Repeat. This continuous cycle is the engine. Good marketers run dozens of iterations per month.

PRINCIPLE 📈

Scalability

When a campaign is profitable (positive ROAS), you scale spend. When it bleeds, you kill it fast. Capital follows proven performance.

Brand vs. Performance: The Key Differences

Dimension Brand Marketing Performance Marketing
Goal Awareness, recall, affinity Clicks, leads, purchases, downloads
Payment Model CPM (per thousand impressions) CPC, CPL, CPA, ROAS
Feedback Speed Weeks to months Hours to days
Risk High (hard to attribute) Lower (real-time kill switch)
Channels TV, OOH, Print, Sponsorships Google, Meta, Programmatic, Email

03 Metrics

Performance Metrics Calculator

Enter your campaign numbers. All metrics calculate live. Understanding what each number means — and what drives it — is the core skill of a performance marketer.

Core Metric Chain
Impressions → CTR → Clicks → CVR → Conversions → Revenue → ROAS
Every metric below derives from this chain. You can improve performance by lifting any link.

Campaign Inputs

Calculated Metrics

CPM — Cost per 1000 Impressions — What you pay per 1000 eyeballs. Lower CPM = cheaper reach.
CPC — Cost per Click — Spend ÷ Clicks. Compare this to your CVR to judge quality.
CPL — Cost per Lead / Conversion — Spend ÷ Conversions. Must be below your AOV or LTV to be profitable.
Total Conversions — Impressions × CTR × CVR. Your output volume.
Total Revenue — Conversions × AOV. Topline return from this campaign.
ROAS — Return on Ad Spend — Revenue ÷ Spend. Industry minimum: 2x. Healthy: 4x+.
LTV:CPA Ratio — LTV ÷ CPL. If 3:1 or better, you're building a healthy business.
The Lever Game Improving CTR by 0.5% or CVR by 1% often outperforms doubling your budget. Before scaling spend, exhaust creative and landing page optimisations first.

05 Attribution

Attribution Models

A customer rarely converts on the first touchpoint. They might see a YouTube ad, then a Meta post, then click a Google search ad, then convert from an email. Which touchpoint gets credit? That's attribution.

Why It Matters Attribution determines which channels you fund and which you cut. Wrong model = wrong budget decisions. A brand running last-click attribution will systematically under-invest in top-funnel channels that do the heavy lifting.

06 Bidding

Bidding Strategies

Bidding is how you compete in ad auctions. Choose wrong and you overpay for bad traffic or underdeliver on potential. Choose right and the algorithm works as your ally.

Google Ad Auction Formula
Ad Rank = Max Bid × Quality Score × Expected Impact of Ad Extensions
Higher Quality Score = cheaper clicks at better positions. Improving QS cuts costs without raising bids.

Manual CPC

You set maximum bid per keyword/ad group. Full control, no automation. High skill ceiling.

Use when Low data volume (<30 conversions/month), new campaigns, niche keywords. Build your baseline here.

Enhanced CPC

Google adjusts your manual bid up/down in real time based on likelihood of conversion. Bridge between manual and smart.

Use when You have some conversion data but want to maintain bid visibility. Good transition strategy.

Target CPA

Set a target cost-per-acquisition. Google's ML optimises bids to hit that CPA across all auctions.

Use when 50+ conversions/month. You know your max acceptable CPA. Great for lead gen.

Target ROAS

Set a revenue/spend ratio target. Algorithm maximises revenue while hitting that ratio.

Use when E-commerce with varied order values. Needs strong conversion tracking with value.

Maximize Conversions

Spend entire budget to get maximum conversion volume, regardless of CPA. Aggressive.

Use when You have budget headroom and want to grow volume fast. Watch your CPA closely.

Maximize Conv. Value

Maximises total conversion value (revenue), not volume. Prioritises higher-value orders.

Use when Different product categories have very different margins. Pair with product-level ROAS targets.
Common Mistake Switching to smart bidding (tCPA / tROAS) too early, before 50+ monthly conversions. The algorithm needs data to learn. Early switching causes a "learning phase" of high CPAs that panics teams into cutting budgets prematurely — starving the algorithm further.

🟡 Headline — Primary Hook

Must stop the scroll in <1 second. Use specificity (number, result, name), not vague claims. "India's Top Masters" adds authority. Headline = promise of value.

Test 3–5 headlines simultaneously. Winner often surprises you.
🟠 Body Copy — Proof + Urgency

Social proof (50,000+ students), benefit stack (Jyotish, Numerology, Vastu), risk reversal (First class FREE). Answers the unspoken question: "Why should I care?"

Lead with the outcome the user wants, not what you offer.
🟢 Display URL — Trust Signal

Users scan the URL to judge credibility. A clean, relevant domain (occultgurukul.com) builds trust. A tracking-heavy URL erodes it.

Customise the path: /astrology-courses not /campaign123
🖼️ Visual — Pattern Interrupt

The image/video is seen before the text. It must create curiosity or emotion that makes the user pause. Native-looking content outperforms polished banner ads in social feeds.

Test UGC-style vs designed creatives. UGC often wins on CTR.
🔵 CTA — Action Command

"Enroll Free →" is specific and low-friction. "Enroll Free" removes risk (free). The arrow signals progression. Generic CTAs like "Learn More" underperform action-specific CTAs.

Match CTA to funnel stage: Free Trial → Register → Buy Now

Creative Frameworks

🔥

PAS Framework

Problem → Agitate → Solution. Identify the pain, amplify it emotionally, then present your offer as the cure.

🌟

AIDA Framework

Attention → Interest → Desire → Action. Classic copywriting model that mirrors the decision journey.

🏆

Social Proof First

Lead with the result (testimonial, review count, case study), then explain the product. Trust is built before the pitch lands.

🎁

Free Offer / Lead Magnet

Lower the barrier with a free resource (ebook, free class, trial). Captures the email for nurture. Works best for considered purchases.

10 Profitability

ROAS & Profitability Simulator

ROAS alone doesn't tell you if you're profitable. A 3x ROAS on a 40% margin product is very different from a 3x ROAS on a 15% margin product. Use this to find your breakeven and true profit.

Breakeven ROAS Formula
Breakeven ROAS = 1 ÷ Gross Margin %
Example: 40% margin product → Breakeven ROAS = 1 ÷ 0.40 = 2.5x. Any ROAS below 2.5x loses money.

11 Lifecycle

Customer Lifecycle Marketing

Acquisition is just the beginning. The real money is in retention. LTV:CAC is the health metric of your entire business. Each lifecycle stage demands a different message, channel, and goal.

1
Stage 01 — Awareness

Stranger → Prospect

The customer doesn't know you exist. Goal is attention and recall. Metrics: CPM, Reach, Video View Rate, Brand Recall Lift. Investment here pays dividends in lower CPC later — branded searches are cheapest.

YouTube TrueView Meta Reach Campaigns Influencer Seeding Programmatic Display
2
Stage 02 — Consideration

Prospect → Engaged Visitor

They know you, now they're researching. Goal: drive intent signals (page visits, video watches, email sign-up). Metrics: CTR, Time on Site, Email Open Rate, Lead Quality Score.

Retargeting Ads Lead Magnets Comparison Content Webinars / Free Demos
3
Stage 03 — Conversion

Prospect → Customer

Decision moment. Remove friction, build confidence, create urgency. Metrics: CVR, Cart Abandonment Rate, CPA, Revenue per Visitor. Your landing page is as important as your ad here.

Google Search (Branded) Abandon Cart Emails Urgency Offers Live Chat / Sales Call
4
Stage 04 — Retention

Customer → Repeat Buyer

Retaining a customer costs 5–7x less than acquiring a new one. Every repeat purchase grows LTV and improves LTV:CAC. Metrics: Repeat Purchase Rate, Churn, Day-30 Retention, NPS.

Post-Purchase Emails Loyalty Programs Upsell / Cross-sell Ads Win-back Campaigns
5
Stage 05 — Advocacy

Customer → Brand Advocate

The holy grail. Advocates refer others (free CAC) and create UGC (free creative). Metrics: NPS, Referral Rate, UGC Volume, Reviews Generated. This stage creates compound growth loops.

Referral Programs Review Campaigns Community Building Partner / Affiliate Seeding
